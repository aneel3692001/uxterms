The Design Glossary

The essential vocabulary of Product, UX, and UI Design. Master the language of the industry.

A/B Testing

Multivariate TestingConversion RateAnalytics

The practice of making websites usable by as many people as possible, including those with disabilities.

Inclusive DesignWCAGContrast Ratio

Adaptive Design

Creating multiple fixed layout sizes that adapt to specific screen sizes rather than fluidly resizing.

Responsive DesignBreakpointsMobile-First

Affordance

A property of an object that indicates how it can be used.

SignifierUI DesignInteraction Design

Agile UX

A design methodology that integrates UX practices into agile development sprints.

Analytics

The systematic computational analysis of data or statistics about user behavior and product performance.

A methodology for creating design systems with five distinct levels: atoms, molecules, organisms, templates, and pages.

Benchmarking

Measuring product performance against competitors or industry standards to identify areas for improvement.

Competitive AnalysisMetricsKPI

Bounce Rate

Breadcrumbs

Call to Action (CTA)

Conversion Funnel

The path users take from awareness to completing a desired action, visualized as a funnel showing drop-off.

Conversion RateUser JourneyAnalytics

Conversion Rate

Crazy Eights

Design Sprint

A five-day process for solving critical business questions through design, prototyping, and testing with users.

Agile UXRapid PrototypingUser Testing

Design System

A collection of reusable components, patterns, and guidelines that ensure consistency across products.

Design Thinking

A human-centered approach to innovation that integrates user needs, technology, and business requirements.

Empathy Map

A visualization tool that articulates what we know about a user, focusing on what they say, think, do, and feel.

User PersonaUser ResearchJourney Map

Error Prevention

Designing interfaces to prevent errors before they occur rather than just providing error messages.

Ethnography

A qualitative research method involving observing users in their natural environment to understand culture and behavior.

Contextual InquiryField StudyUser Research

Eye Tracking

A research method that measures where and how long users look at different parts of an interface.

F-PatternZ-PatternUsability Testing

F-Pattern

A scanning pattern where users read the top line, then a bit of the middle, then scan down the left side.

Feature Flag

A development technique that allows designers and developers to toggle features on or off, enabling safe testing of new designs in production.

A/B TestingContinuous DeploymentExperimentation

Feedback Loop

A system where outputs are routed back as inputs, creating continuous improvement cycles in design.

IterationContinuous ImprovementAgile UX

Fidelity

The level of detail and functionality in a prototype, ranging from low (sketches) to high (pixel-perfect).

PrototypeWireframeMockup

Fitts's Law

A predictive model stating that the time to acquire a target is a function of the distance to and size of the target.

Interaction DesignUsabilityTouch Target

Five Second Test

A usability test where users view a design for five seconds and then recall what they remember.

First ImpressionUsability TestingUser Research

Flat Design

A minimalist design approach that emphasizes usability and clean aesthetics without 3D effects.

Flow State

User EngagementGamificationUX Psychology

Focus Group

User ResearchQualitative ResearchInterview

Visual DesignLayoutTypography

Graceful Degradation

A structure of columns and rows used to organize content and create visual consistency.

LayoutResponsive DesignVisual Design

Guerrilla Testing

Usability TestingLean UXUser Research

Hick's Law

The time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices.

Cognitive LoadDecision FatigueChoice Paradox

High-Fidelity Prototype

A detailed, interactive prototype that closely resembles the final product in appearance and functionality.

PrototypeFidelityMockup
